Task 2 (3 points). Sea Day

Sea Day is celebrated on the 3rd Monday of July. Create a formula that shows the date of Sea Day in any given year (the user can input the year into a certain cell, and the formula shows the Sea Day date in another cell).

Check your solution using this table.

Make sure that your formula works correctly if the first day of July is Monday (such as in 2013).

Hints:

  1. The function =DATE(year, month, day) converts three numbers representing year, month, and day to a single serial number that represents a date in Excel.

  2. The function =WEEKDAY(serial_number) returns an integer [1..7] corresponding to the day of week for the given serial_number date.

  3. To calculate a date after d days from the given date serial_number, use the expression serial_number + d.

  4. Excel displays serial numbers as date strings (such as “2018年6月1日”).

  5. It might be useful to find the day of week of the 1st of July of the given year.

Task 3 (3 points). Doctoral Students v2

Here is a shortened excerpt from the University of Aizu regulations on PhD students:

Students must satisfy either one of the requirements below for conferment of a doctoral degree: (A) the student has two or more papers accepted by academic journals prior to the final dissertation review, at least one of which must have been accepted by a journal which is recognized as a major journal, or (B) the student has one paper accepted by a major journal, and has at least 2 examined papers which have been published in proceedings of major international conferences.

Prepare an Excel table with three cells where a student can enter the following numbers:

  1. The number of papers published in proceedings of major international conferences.
  2. The number of papers published in major journals.
  3. The total number of papers published in journals.

Create a formula that prints “Yes” or “No” depending on whether the student satisfies the requirements for conferment a doctoral degree.

Task 4 (2 points). Correlations v2

Prepare a table of three columns:

  1. Japanese prefecture names.
  2. The number of convenience stores per 100 000 people.
  3. The number of ski & snowboard users per 100 people..

Use data from these pages: Number of Ski & Snowboard Players and Convenience Stores.

Is there any correlation between these data series? Calculate correlation coefficient and create a scatter graph showing the relations.
